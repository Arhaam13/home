Almost all cardists bring out their own decks of cards, which means there are so many choices to choose from, and we will help you cut down on which you want to use. Here are the top 14 decks of all time (quality- wise for magic purposes):

Poker 808 Bicycle Cards

This first choice may seem incredibly obvious, but Bicycle does have some of the most high-quality, decent cards on the market. These Poker 808 playing cards are the standard choice from Bicycle that you would see most often in red and blue.They are a tested and loved the pack, with customers raving about how incredibly durable they are even though they are thin and easy to shuffle. The cards have a glossy finish on them, making them stronger and less resistant to stains from liquids. On top of that, they are said to take quite a while to lose their snap – customers had mentioned in online reviews that they got over 90-100 hours before it was lost. These playing cards are also very affordable. You can buy a two-pack combo (comes with both a red and blue deck) on Amazon for only $8+ tax!

Pros

  1. High-quality
  2. Durability
  3. Affordable

Cons

  1. Old-fashion design

Swe deck

Next up, we have the SWE deck. To give you a little background about this deck of playing cards, they are named after the author S.W. Erdnase – he published The Expert at the Card Table in the year 1902. This book told the world how magicians were able to pull off their sleight of hand and is now a staple for anyone in the magic community. These cards have the entire cover of the book on the back of them. The cards are a rich and vibrant green and gold, and very easy to read. They are also a fantastic conversation starter when using them around other people. They are a limited-edition card from Ellusionist, so they will be a little more difficult to purchase than the Bicycle cards we just looked at.

Pros

  1. Rich and vibrent design
  2. Limited- edition

Cons

  1. Difficult to purchase

Absinthe Playing cards V2

Another great option for playing cards if you like the aesthetic of green ones are the Absinthe Playing Cards V2. Just like the famed alcohol Absinthe, these cards are an electric green that will catch the eyes of those watching you perform. This deck was originally only available as part of the Prohibition box set with six different decks in it. However, you can now purchase it all on its own. They are thin and incredibly easy to work with for both shuffling and performing tricks.

Pros

  1. Eye-catchig design
  2. Incredibly easy to shuffle

Cons

  1. Edge of card may be delaminated after a few hours of use

Bicycle 52 proof

The Bicycle 52 Proof playing cards are an excellent choice for anyone in the magic community who enjoys the aesthetic of vintage cards in the 1920s. With the regular Bicycle layout and the old-time script and design, these are a gorgeous choice for your magic tricks. They are thick and sturdy cards, while also remaining incredibly easy to handle and use. The print and images are easy for you to read as well.

Pros

  1. Thick and sturdy
  2. Easy to handle and use
  3. Prints are easy to read

Cons

  1. Some cards don't have a gold foil

Sleepers

These cards are a hit if you are more of a minimalist. The Sleepers deck of playing cards is simply black, with red only on the hearts and the diamonds. It also looks neat and intricate when the deck is shuffled as well. The cards were designed by Oban Jones and were based on a concept by Daniel Madison. These will cost you a little more than most of the other options on the list, mostly because they are a limited edition and are a little more difficult to find.

Pros

  1. Minimalist
  2. Neat and intricate

Cons

  1. Costs a little more

Bicycle Black Ghost

The Bicycle Black Ghost cards are a great, unique look for any magician. The sleek black cards are very simple and beautiful, and the Jokers are one of a kind, too. These playing cards are incredibly durable, as they are made with a thick, firm card stock. They also have a nice glide finish, making them easy and efficient to handle.

Pros

  1. Unique look for magicians
  2. Simple and beautiful
  3. Durable and easy to handle

Cons

  1. Pakaging box isn't so good

Pipmen Playing Cards

Want something really special? Pipmen playing cards are incredibly unique in their design. Each card has a different, surreal image on it, making them a lot of fun to pick up and go through. As far as quality goes, the company uses 310GSM embossed Black Core European card stock to make the product. They also apply casino-grade printing techniques. The design was made to work well in humidity and last through extremely frequent usage, and their Emerald finish coating is fantastic for shuffling, dealing, and tricks.

Pros

  1. Unique design
  2. Uses 310GSM material
  3. With casino grade printing techniques

Cons

  1. More expensave than other brands

Ellusionist Fathom Playing Cards

These gorgeous cards were specifically designed with magicians in mind. They feature a lovely oceanic theme that was inspired by Jules Verne’s novel 20000 Leagues Under the Sea. The deck is extremely high-quality. They are made from USPCC stock and a soft-glide performance finish – this makes them perfect for sleight of hand techniques. They are also one part of a four-part set called the Origin Series made by Ellusionist. Each set is for a different element, this one, of course, being water. Even though they are a watery blue, they are still very easy to read and use for tricks.

Pros

  1. Designed for magicians
  2. Extremly high- quality deck
  3. Very easy to read and use for tricks

Cons

  1. Bubbly design may not be appealing

Bumblebee Playing Cards

Looking for unique cards that also have a nice, vintage look to them? Bumble Bee playing cards are perfect, and they also donate a portion of the proceeds towards saving bees. They were specifically designed for sleight of hand tricks, as well as to bring support to the honey bees. The design is very high-quality. The card stock is thick and stiff with a nice finish.

Pros

  1. Unique vintage design
  2. Designed for sleight of hand tricks
  3. Very high- quality

Cons

  1. Digits are difficult to read

Theory 11 union playing cards

If you are interested in American history, this is the deck of playing cards for you. The Theory11 Union deck is a patriotic product based on the history of the United States. -They are made out of FSC-certified paper derived from sustainable forests using vegetable-based inks. The cards also have a signature finish on them, making them perfect for easy handling, shuffling, and tricks. The price is fair as well.

Pros

  3. Affordable

Cons

Joker and the theif: White gold edition

This gorgeous custom deck is perfect for magicians. The crisp white and gold edition of the Joker and the Thief playing cards are beautiful and unique to anything else on the market. These were specifically designed by an artist for cardists, magicians, players, and collectors. They are of the highest quality and incredibly durable with casino-grade paper stock and finish. They also have a durable linen classic finish coating and hot foil stamping on both sides of the card. They are a little pricier than a lot of the options on the list, but you are getting a gorgeous and unique product.

Pros
